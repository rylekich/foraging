week 1

Self as source OR the endless well of content within

When I think about self as source I’m curious about what our creative brains are working on when all the debris and constraints of trying to assimilate into the world around us are removed. What would we invent if we had nothing in the way? I believe that there is some magical part of us generating content whether we are bringing it to life or not and that it is an endless well continually renewing, having non-verbal conversations with itself and once in a while we have access to the fun.

Warm Up

Listen to Week #1 Warm-up Audio

    Audio Transcript

  • Start by locating your favorite organ, bone, muscle, tissue or system and move from there for a little while.
  • Now imagine you are on a vast plain. Soft breeze is kissing your skin and there is space expanding all around you. The ground is firm and wide under your feet.
  • Now imagine this wide, open plain is inside you.
  • Now turn on a stopwatch and 5 minutes move in ways that feel god, feel friendly and familiar.
  • Now for 5 minutes dance in ways you hate, that embarrass and annoy you.
  • Now for 5 minutes dance like your grandparents.
  • Now for 2 minutes make shapes.
  • Now for 2 minutes be your favorite animal, plant, reptile, bird or aquatic creature.
  • Now for 1 minute be the color yellow.
  • Now for 1 minute be shimmery
  • Now for 1 minute shake your entire body

Exercises

  • Pick 10 minutes of your day to be “art”
  • For 15 minutes make a 1 min long dance
  • Make sure it includes: something you love, something you hate, something lovely, something grotesque, something with time, something without time, a fruit or vegetable
  • Record your dance. Watch it. Identify something important and take it out.
  • Do it again. Record it. Watch it. Take out another important thing.
  • Do it again. Record it. Watch it.
  • Put these 2 important things back in in different places and see what happens.
  • Record it. Watch it.
  • Change it until you like it.
  • Do this new version in a costume. Record it. Watch it.
